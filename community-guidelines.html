Community Guidelines

Red Hat Society community guidelines and code of conduct for Red Hatters WA members and online interactions.

Online Platform Code of Conduct for the Sisterhood

Our community guidelines help ensure that all Red Hat Society members can enjoy a positive, welcoming, and fun environment both online and in person. These guidelines reflect our core values of fun, friendship, and sisterhood.

1

Be Kind and Positive

Hatting is about fun and friendship, be positive and kind to one another. Disrespectful comments or posts will be deleted and the author might be removed.

2

Be Inclusive, Not Exclusive

Posts in any chapter should be relevant to all members of that chapter. We welcome women of all walks of life and backgrounds.

3

No Hate Speech or Bullying

No bullying of any kind, or degrading comments will be tolerated. It is our mission to make everyone feel safe and welcome.

4

No Personal Attacks on People or Events

If you have a complaint, please bring it to the attention of your friends at Hatquarters. We encourage constructive communication and problem-solving.

5

No Soliciting

Please do not solicit Members on this platform. Sales posts will be deleted and the offender may be removed.

Member Guidelines

For over 20 years, the Red Hat Society enjoys a reputation as a worldwide, lively, free flowing – no rules – sort of community. But the truth is we would not be a thriving, global Sisterhood if we didn't have a few guidelines. We strive to be inclusive and welcome women of all walks of life. Hatquarters is proud to have always limited our guidelines to a select few, giving our Queens and members plenty of room for individuality.

Dues Payment

Queens and members pay dues. Dues are paid online, by phone or mailed to the RHS Hatquarters located at 369 S. Acacia Avenue, Fullerton, CA 92831, USA. This revenue is sent to cover the costs of maintaining, developing and providing programs for the benefit of members of RHS.

Queen Support

RHS Queens support the Society by ensuring that all of the women who play within their chapter pay annual dues to the RHS. Members join the Society first as a member of the Official Red Hat Society Chapter and then, if they wish, they may align with other local and/or online chapters.

Event Attire

Whether hosted by Hatquarters, an online or local chapter, RHS events are intended for our official members. At RHS events, Queens and members wear the Society's signature colors of red and purple (for those 50 and beyond) and pink and lavender (for those under 50). How a Queen or member interprets the use of our colors is at her discretion and fun needs to be at the center of it all.

Reporting and Enforcement

We take our community guidelines seriously and have systems in place to ensure they are followed. If you encounter behavior that violates these guidelines, please report it using the appropriate channels.

Report Inappropriate Content

If you see content that violates our guidelines, use the report function available on our platform. Reports are reviewed by our moderation team.

Block Members

You can block members who are causing issues. This will prevent you from seeing their posts and them from contacting you.

Contact Hatquarters

For serious violations or ongoing issues, contact Hatquarters directly at info@redhatsociety.com or call 1-714-738-0001.

Our Mission

The Red Hat Society is built on the foundation of fun, friendship, and sisterhood. Our community guidelines are designed to protect and nurture these core values while ensuring that every member feels welcome, safe, and valued.

Fun

We believe in celebrating life with joy, laughter, and a sense of adventure.

Friendship

We foster meaningful connections and lifelong bonds between women.

Sisterhood

We support and uplift each other through all of life's adventures.
