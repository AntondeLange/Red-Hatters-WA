Chapter Organisation & Administration

Comprehensive guides for running successful Red Hat Society chapters

Fundraising Ideas

Generally speaking, Hatters don't do fundraising. However there may be times when chapters want to raise money to help a particular charity, or to raise chapter funds for convention costuming or other chapter needs.

Fines

Charge a $2 fine for forgetting to wear a hat or name-badge, or for being late

Craft Auctions

If you have crafty members who can create something like hats, cards, jewellery or even baked goods, you could auction these off. Either splitting the profits with the person donating the items, or with the chapter keeping all profits.

Bake Sale

Have members bake cupcakes, muffins, slices and other individual portioned foods, then come together for a bake sale where everyone buys a cupcake etc. for a nominated price per item (eg $1/$2 each) with proceeds going to the chapter.

Cupcake Decorating

Everyone pays $2 to decorate their cupcake, with decorating items provided by the chapter (eg red and purple frosting, choc chips and other fun things). The chapter then keeps any profits made.

Book Sale

Members bring old books they no longer need and these are then sold off for $2 each or auctioned to raise money.

Lucky Dip

Have a selection of inexpensive items wrapped up, and charge $1-$2 per go.

Raffle Auction

(AKA Penny sale) Each member buys a set of raffle tickets or coupons (eg 10 tickets for $1), and there are several items to raffle off, each with a bowl next to them. Members put their raffle tickets into the bowls of the items they want (they can choose to put one ticket in the bowl or several tickets if they really want it) â then each item has the winner drawn from the bowls.

Mystery Auction

Everyone brings a wrapped gift worth $5 or $10, in a particular theme. People then bid on the mystery package without knowing what is inside it. You could set a maximum bid for each item so that it doesn't go beyond a certain limit. The chapter gets to keep all raised funds.

$2 Raffle

Have a prize worth $2 and everyone puts $2 into a jar as their entry to the raffle, and their name goes into a hat. The person who gets drawn from the hat gets the prize, and they then buy the prize for the next month raffle.

Lolly Jar

Have a jar of lollies and everyone pays $2 to have a guess at how many lollies are in the jar. The winner gets to keep the lollies and the chapter keeps the money.

Cook Book

Have members send in their favourite tried and true recipes, gather them together, then have them printed and bound. Sell the books to cover the cost of printing plus extra funds for the chapter.

Car Wash

Have members bring their cars along and pay a fee to have their car washed (bonus if you can rope in husbands/children to wash the cars, then all the Hatters can gather around relaxing while their cars are being washed!)

Hosting an Event

You may like to hold a larger get-together with ladies from other local chapters, or open it up to invite any Hatters from around Victoria or even around Australia. Running a larger event can be a bit daunting so here are some tips:

Choosing a Date

Pick a date that doesn't conflict with any existing Hatter events or any public holidays. Make sure to check the upcoming events and our Facebook Group events. If your event is a large one that interstate Hatters might like to attend, make sure it's not on when any other large events are on (in any State)

Venues

Choose a venue that has good parking and ideally good facilities for those with mobility issues and access to public transport. If serving food, check they can cater for food intolerances.

Picnic

Attendees bring their own food, and you can find a park that won't cost money to hire. Be aware that you'll need toilets and shade might also be good to have. Check insurance issues and if any council regulations apply.

Restaurant Meal

Attendees will pay for their own meal and drinks. The venue will have insurance, making this a nice easy way to have an event. Although numbers might be limited due to space. Also make sure to choose restaurants that will allow each guest to pay for their own meal (usually "clubs" do this, smaller restaurants usually do not)

Private Room/Venue

These usually require you to pay a fee to hire the space, and you'll also pay a price per head for each meal. When doing this, you will need to make sure you cover your costs. Make sure you add up how much the venue hire costs, plus any decorations you need to buy, and any other expenses (not including the food costs at this point). If you are including goodies/prizes, factor those in too.

Pacing

Not all Hatters can cope with a lot of physical activity or a long event. So if you have a multiple day event, you may need to schedule a rest time between morning and evening events. Some ladies might want to go back to their rooms to freshen up and change clothes.

If you have an event where attendees will need to move from one building/activity to another, make sure to allow plenty of time for those who walk slowly. If you are having games and activities, make sure to allow plenty of time for everyone to move around and get ready.

Give all the Information

Make sure your attendees know everything they need: Cost, date, time, when payment is due, how to make payment (on the day or in advance), dresscode, expectations (eg if there will be anything unusual like walking/stairs/games), who is running it â and anything else you think they should know beforehand.

Depending on the venue, giving information on parking, accommodation options in the area and other such things can also be very helpful for your guests.

Advance Notice

Give plenty of advanced warning/notice of the event (even post a "saving the date" if you have a date in mind but no further details ready) â so everyone has time to prepare (including you!!).

This allows your attendees to keep the date free, set money aside and it also allows other Chapters to know when your event is when planning their own to avoid conflicts.

Public Liability Insurance

If you do not have a policy to cover your chapter, then you may wish to choose venues that have their own insurance cover. Check liabilities of hosting in public parks and at member homes etc.

Format of your event notices

  • Saving your event flyer/promo as an image is the best way for it to be shared in a way everyone can view it properly.
  • Flyers as a .doc or .docx can be unreadable for some Hatters, and often any layout and fonts you use will not look the same when opened on the other person's computer/laptop/phone. So this is not recommended for event notices.
  • Save your .doc files document as a .pdf file instead to preserve the same look and make it readable.

Easy & Pretty Way to Make Event Flyers

The easiest way to make a great looking flyer that you can save as an image is to use www.Canva.com (which is a free online tool)

This allows you to make great looking event flyers (and other things), using graphics and templates they provide, and you can move things around and add your own text. You can then download the creation as an image or a .pdf (or both!).

Registration/Booking Forms

Some event hosts like to provide a registration form where attendees have to fill out their details and sign a waiver â rather than simply having the attendees email or phone to RSVP.

While you may only need to know their name so you can make sure you know how many (and who) is attending, it is also a good idea to have at least their phone number and email address in case you need to contact them to inform them of any changes to the event.

Important Security Note

You should NEVER request or send credit card information via email, facebook, messenger, whatsapp etc. These are NOT secure enough to transmit sensitive information such as credit card information! Send this information over the phone instead.

Indemnity Waivers

Indemnity forms added with booking forms can be problematic for the reasons stated above with booking forms.

An alternative to having Hatters complete a waiver at registration may be to have as part of the booking process wording that says that by booking and attending this event, you agree to the terms of the waiver. So long as that's clear, then all attendees have agreed to your terms by the fact they are at the event.

A sign-in sheet at the event that includes a waiver may also be a good option regardless of whether your booking form includes one or not â so all those attending have to sign in, which also signs the waiver, as well as providing you with a list of those who actually attended.

How to Start a Chapter

Starting a Red Hat Society chapter is an exciting journey! Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Find Your Passion

    Make sure you're passionate about the Red Hat Society and its mission of celebrating life, friendship, and fun for women over 50.

  2. Gather Your Founding Members

    You'll need at least 5 members to officially start a chapter. Look for friends, family members, or acquaintances who share your enthusiasm.

  3. Choose Your Chapter Name

    Select a creative, memorable name that reflects your chapter's personality and local area. Consider names that are fun, unique, and easy to remember.

  4. Register with Red Hat Society

    Visit the official Red Hat Society website to register your chapter and receive your official charter and materials.

  5. Plan Your First Meeting

    Organize an initial gathering to introduce members, discuss chapter goals, and plan future activities.

  6. Establish Chapter Guidelines

    Create basic rules and expectations for your chapter, including meeting frequency, dress codes, and member responsibilities.

  7. Plan Regular Activities

    Schedule regular "Hoots" (gatherings) and special events to keep members engaged and build strong friendships.

Remember: It's All About Fun!

The Red Hat Society is about celebrating life, making friends, and having fun. Don't overthink the process â focus on creating a welcoming, joyful environment for your members.

How to Name Your Chapter

Choosing the right name for your chapter is an important part of establishing your identity. Here are some tips and ideas:

Naming Guidelines

  • Be Creative: Choose a name that reflects your chapter's personality and local area
  • Keep it Memorable: Select something that's easy to remember and fun to say
  • Make it Unique: Ensure your name stands out and isn't too similar to existing chapters
  • Consider Local Elements: Incorporate local landmarks, history, or regional characteristics
  • Keep it Appropriate: Ensure the name is suitable for all ages and backgrounds

Name Ideas by Category

Location-Based

Riverside Red Hatters, Mountain View Matriarchs, Coastal Queens, Valley Vixens

Nature-Inspired

Rose Garden Royals, Wildflower Warriors, Sunset Sirens, Blossom Belles

Elegant & Royal

Diamond Divas, Crown Jewels, Royal Roses, Pearl Princesses

Fun & Playful

Giggle Gals, Chuckle Chicks, Merry Matriarchs, Joyful Jesters

Magical & Mystical

Enchanted Elegance, Mystical Mavens, Fairy Godmothers, Wishing Well Women

Food & Drink

Tea Time Tarts, Coffee Klatch Queens, Wine & Dine Divas, Sweet Treat Sisters

Brainstorming Tips

  • Hold a naming party where all members can contribute ideas
  • Create a list of words that describe your chapter's personality
  • Consider your local area's history, landmarks, or unique features
  • Think about what makes your chapter special or different
  • Test potential names with friends and family for feedback

How to Attract New Members to Your Chapter

Growing your chapter with new members is essential for its success and longevity. Here are effective strategies to attract new Hatters:

Word of Mouth

Your best recruitment tool is your existing members! Encourage them to invite friends, family members, and acquaintances who might enjoy the Red Hat Society experience.

Community Involvement

Public Events

Participate in community festivals, fairs, and events wearing your red hats and purple outfits. This creates visibility and curiosity.

Local Media

Submit articles to local newspapers about your chapter's activities and community involvement. Many papers love featuring local groups.

Social Media

Create a Facebook page or Instagram account for your chapter. Share photos and stories of your activities to attract interest.

Local Businesses

Ask local businesses if you can display flyers or business cards. Many are happy to support community groups.

Open House Events

Host special "Open House" events where potential members can attend a meeting or activity to see what your chapter is all about. This gives them a chance to meet current members and experience the fun firsthand.

Recruitment Strategies

  • Be Approachable: Always wear a smile and be welcoming to newcomers
  • Share Your Story: Tell people about the friendships and fun you've found in the Red Hat Society
  • Offer Trial Memberships: Let potential members attend a few meetings before committing
  • Create Inviting Materials: Develop attractive flyers and brochures about your chapter
  • Partner with Other Groups: Collaborate with other women's organizations for joint events

Remember: Quality Over Quantity

It's better to have a smaller group of committed, enthusiastic members than a large group of people who aren't truly engaged. Focus on finding women who share your passion for fun, friendship, and the Red Hat Society spirit.

Welcoming a New Member

Making new members feel welcome and included is crucial for their success in your chapter. Here's how to create a warm, inviting experience:

First Meeting Experience

Personal Greeting

Have a designated "Welcome Committee" member greet new arrivals personally and introduce them to other members.

Name Tags

Provide clear name tags for everyone, including the new member, to help with introductions and remember names.

Welcome Gift

Consider giving a small welcome gift like a Red Hat Society pin, bookmark, or small trinket to make them feel special.

Information Packet

Provide a packet with chapter information, meeting schedule, contact details, and Red Hat Society basics.

Integration Strategies

  • Buddy System: Assign a current member as a "buddy" to help the new member navigate their first few meetings
  • Ice Breakers: Include fun ice breaker activities that help everyone get to know each other
  • Seat Them Strategically: Place new members with friendly, outgoing members who will include them in conversations
  • Explain Traditions: Take time to explain any chapter-specific traditions or inside jokes
  • Ask for Input: Encourage new members to share their ideas and suggestions

Follow-Up

Don't let the welcome end after the first meeting. Follow up with new members to ensure they're feeling comfortable and answer any questions they might have.

Creating an Inclusive Environment

  • Make sure everyone has a chance to speak and be heard
  • Avoid inside jokes that might make newcomers feel excluded
  • Be patient with new members as they learn the ropes
  • Celebrate their unique contributions and perspectives
  • Remember that everyone was new once â treat them as you would want to be treated

Success Tip

The key to successful new member integration is making them feel like they belong from day one. A warm welcome and ongoing support will help them become active, engaged members of your chapter.
