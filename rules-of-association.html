Rules of Association

Official Rules of Association for the Australian Red Hat Society Western Australia (ARGHA-WA)

1. Name and Objects

1.1 Name

The name of the association is "Australian Red Hat Society Western Australia" (ARGHA-WA).

1.2 Objects

The objects for which the association is established are:

  • To promote friendship, fun, and sisterhood among women in Western Australia
  • To organize and conduct social activities, events, and gatherings for members
  • To support charitable causes and community service initiatives
  • To foster personal growth, creativity, and self-expression among members
  • To maintain connections with the international Red Hat Society community
  • To provide educational and recreational opportunities for members

2. Membership

2.1 Eligibility

Membership is open to all women who:

  • Are 18 years of age or older
  • Reside in Western Australia or have a strong connection to the state
  • Support the objects and values of the association
  • Pay the prescribed membership fees

2.2 Application Process

Applications for membership must be submitted in writing to the committee and include:

  • Completed membership application form
  • Payment of membership fees
  • Agreement to abide by these rules and the association's code of conduct

2.3 Membership Categories

The association recognizes the following membership categories:

  • Full Members: Women aged 50 and over who wear red and purple
  • Pink Ladies: Women under 50 who wear pink and lavender
  • Honorary Members: Individuals recognized for exceptional service to the association

3. Management Committee

3.1 Composition

The management committee consists of:

  • President (Queen Mother)
  • Vice-President (Vice Queen Mother)
  • Secretary
  • Treasurer
  • Up to 6 additional committee members

3.2 Election and Term

Committee members are elected at the annual general meeting and hold office for a term of two years, with half the committee retiring each year.

3.3 Powers and Duties

The committee has the power to:

  • Manage the affairs of the association
  • Appoint sub-committees as necessary
  • Make decisions on behalf of the association between general meetings
  • Prepare annual reports and financial statements

4. Meetings

4.1 Annual General Meeting

The association holds an annual general meeting within 6 months of the end of each financial year. The meeting includes:

  • Presentation of annual report and financial statements
  • Election of committee members
  • Consideration of any special business

4.2 Special General Meetings

Special general meetings may be called by the committee or by written request of at least 10% of members.

4.3 Quorum

The quorum for general meetings is 10 members or 25% of the membership, whichever is less.

5. Financial Management

5.1 Financial Year

The financial year runs from 1 July to 30 June each year.

5.2 Membership Fees

Annual membership fees are determined by the committee and approved at the annual general meeting. Fees are due on 1 July each year.

5.3 Financial Records

The association maintains proper financial records and prepares annual financial statements in accordance with applicable accounting standards.

6. Conduct and Discipline

6.1 Code of Conduct

All members are expected to:

  • Conduct themselves in a manner that reflects positively on the association
  • Treat all members with respect and dignity
  • Participate in activities in a spirit of fun and friendship
  • Abide by the association's community guidelines

6.2 Disciplinary Procedures

Members who breach the code of conduct may be subject to:

  • Verbal warning
  • Written warning
  • Suspension from activities
  • Termination of membership

7. Dissolution

7.1 Dissolution Process

The association may be dissolved by special resolution at a general meeting. Any surplus assets after payment of debts and liabilities must be distributed to a charitable organization with similar objects.

8. Amendments

8.1 Amendment Process

These rules may only be amended by special resolution at a general meeting. Members must be given at least 21 days' notice of any proposed amendments.

9. Interpretation

9.1 Governing Law

These rules are governed by the laws of Western Australia and the Associations Incorporation Act 2015.

9.2 Definitions

In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires:

  • "Association" means the Australian Red Hat Society Western Australia
  • "Committee" means the management committee of the association
  • "Member" means a person who has been admitted to membership of the association
  • "Special resolution" means a resolution passed by at least 75% of members present and voting
