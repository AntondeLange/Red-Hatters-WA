History of the Red Hat Society

From a simple birthday gift to a worldwide movement of fun, friendship, and frivolity

The Beginning

In November 1997, Sue Ellen Cooper purchased a red fedora hat from a thrift shop and gave it along with a copy of "The Warning" poem to her 55-year-old friend as a birthday gift. This simple gesture would change the lives of millions of women worldwide.

1997

Birth of the Red Hat Society

On April 25th, 1998, Sue Ellen and a group of 5 friends got together for an afternoon tea while wearing red hats with purple clothing. This gathering marked the official birth of the "Red Hat Society" and established April 25th as "Red Hat Society Day."

1998

Pink Hatters Introduced

In 2000, the custom of ladies under 50 wearing pink hats with lavender clothing was introduced, creating the "Pink Hatters" and making the society accessible to women of all ages.

2000

Global Expansion

By 2004, there were 40,000 chapters worldwide! The movement had grown from a small group of friends to a global phenomenon, bringing joy and friendship to women across the world.

2004

Cultural Impact

In 2005, Red Hat ladies were featured on the popular TV show "The Simpsons," bringing the society into mainstream culture and introducing it to millions of viewers worldwide.

2005

Historical Recognition

In 2011, the first red hat was put on display in the Smithsonian Museum of American History, cementing the Red Hat Society's place in American cultural history.

2011

Hatting in Australia

Florence Slattery, The Supreme Matriarch of Rose City Red Hatters Warwick Queensland, was the first to bring the red hat concept of love, laughter and friendship to Australia. It is said she saw Red Hatters while on a trip to the US and decided to start a chapter here.

Florence received her membership certificate on the 25th July 2001, and celebrated her 100th birthday on the 22nd June 2016. Sadly, Florence passed away on the 5th March 2017, but her legacy lives on in the thousands of Australian women who continue to embrace the Red Hat Society's values.

Due to the official RHS day being April 25th (which in Australia is ANZAC Day), many Australian Hatters feel it is inappropriate to celebrate Hatting on this day. So many instead choose to celebrate "Aussie Red Hat Day" on July 25th, to both honour the legacy of Florence starting Hatting in Australia as well as celebrating the Red Hat Society itself.
